Concept Design · Onboarding Reimagined
How I'd make the 30-minute wait
feel like the beginning of something.
Doji's avatar takes ~30 min to generate. Right now that's a notification and a loading screen. Here's how to turn that wait into delight — and get users invested before their avatar even arrives.
01
Capture
Six selfies. Two minutes.
Your entire likeness.
Current pain: users don't know what makes a good avatar photo. Bad inputs = bad avatar = churn. The fix is a real-time selfie coach inside the app — like Snapchat's face scan but fashion-grade. Each photo slot only unlocks once lighting and angle quality are confirmed. Fewer bad photos, better avatars, better first impressions.

02
Your Style DNA
Tell us who you are.
We'll start building
before you wait.
The avatar takes 30 minutes. But taste is instant. While diffusion models work in the background, users pick their aesthetics, favourite brands, and style eras — and Doji starts curating their feed immediately. When the avatar is ready, it lands in a world already built for them.
✓ Feed ready before avatar
✓ Reduces perceived wait
✓ Stronger first session
03
The Wait
30 minutes.
But it doesn't
feel like waiting.
The biggest drop-off risk is the 30-minute avatar generation window. Instead of just a loading bar, show a tabbed "while you wait" experience — style tips from brands in their feed, a lookbook from their chosen aesthetics, and a running commentary from Doji about what your photos revealed about your style. Curiosity keeps them. Anticipation sells the avatar.

04
The Reveal
Not a notification.
A moment.
The avatar reveal should feel like something. Not just a push notification — a return screen with your likeness front and centre, your first curated look already on it, and a subtle message: "Your style story starts now." The 30 minutes of anticipation pays off here. It's a product moment worth designing.
